What it is

A NEW ONE is a permissionless memecoin launchpad. Anyone can create a token for free (you pay only network gas), and every token trades immediately on its own constant-product bonding curve priced in USDC. There is no order book, no seeding liquidity, and no listing step — the contract itself is the market maker.

rug-proof

No LP to pull

Reserves are locked inside the contract for the token's whole life. There is no liquidity pool a dev can drain and no migration step.

USDC-native

One asset

On Arc, USDC pays gas and buys tokens. No volatile gas coin, no priority-fee auctions.

fair launch

Anti-snipe

A per-wallet cap in the first blocks blunts bots and bundlers sniping a launch.

The bonding curve

Each token has a fixed supply of 1,000,000,000 (1B), all placed on the curve at creation. Price follows a constant-product formula against a virtual USDC reserve, so every buy pushes the price up and every sell pushes it down — smoothly and predictably.

  • Market cap = current price × 1B supply. The app shows this (not a raw price) so numbers mean something.
  • Graduation is a milestone badge at a set amount of USDC raised. Trading continues on the same curve — there is no forced migration.
  • Buys and sells always clear against the contract, so there is always liquidity to exit.

Fees & creator earnings

Every trade pays a flat 1% fee, split down the middle:

That's a bigger day-one creator cut than most launchpads. If a creator doesn't claim within 7 days, the pot rolls into platform fees (anyone can trigger the sweep) — so claim your earnings.

Safety by design

  • Rug-proof: no LP exists, and curve reserves can never be withdrawn — only traded against. Fees are held in separate accounting from reserves.
  • Anti-snipe: for the first 20 blocks after launch, each wallet can buy at most 2% of supply.
  • Verified & open: the contract is public and the frontend is a static, no-backend page you can inspect. Token names, images and comments are escaped, so a hostile token can't attack you.

Launch a token

  1. Connect a wallet (or sign in with Google — see Wallets).
  2. Click + Insert Coin.
  3. Set name, symbol, and optionally an image (JPG/PNG, ≤5 MB, ≥100×100 — auto-cropped & stored on-chain), a description and socials (X / Telegram / website).
  4. Optionally add a small dev buy at launch (still subject to the anti-snipe cap).
  5. Confirm. You pay only gas — launching is free. Your token is live and tradable instantly.
Metadata is stored fully on-chain, so your token's image and links can't rot or be swapped out from under holders.

Trade

  • Open any token to see its market cap, holders, 24h volume, age and a live price chart.
  • Buy or sell with an amount or the 25 / 50 / 75 / MAX presets.
  • Set a max slippage (1 / 2 / 5% or custom) — the contract enforces a minimum-output so a fast-moving curve can't fill you far from the quote.
  • Your position card shows real cost basis and P&L using a live sell quote (fees + curve impact included) — not an optimistic price × balance.
  • Discover coins via Trending / Newest / Top MC / Graduating, search, advanced filters, a live trade feed, and a watchlist.

Wallets & funding

Connect

Use any injected wallet — MetaMask, OKX, Rabby — or Continue with Google to instantly create a non-custodial embedded wallet with no seed phrase. Both paths behave identically once connected.

Fund it

A fresh wallet needs USDC on Arc (it's the gas and the money). On testnet, the app shows a Get test USDC button that opens Circle's faucet (20 USDC every 2h, pick Arc Testnet). The funding panel disappears automatically once the balance lands.

Arc mainnet is not live yet (targeted by Circle for 2026). On testnet, tokens have no real value — it's for trying the platform.

Trust signals

Every token page carries an honest risk read-out so you can judge before you buy:

  • 🔒 Rug-proof — structural, always true, links the verified contract.
  • Dev holds % — the creator's real balance vs circulating supply (green <5%, amber 5–20%, red ≥20%).
  • Top wallet % and Snipers % — concentration estimated from trades (labelled, since direct transfers aren't counted).
  • Creator profile & reputation score — a wallet's past launches, graduation rate and whether they exited their own tokens.
  • On-chain comments — a per-token thread; you must hold the token (or be its creator) to post, so it resists comment-bot spam.

FAQ

Is it really rug-proof?

There is no liquidity pool and reserves can't be withdrawn from the contract — only traded against. So the classic "pull the LP" rug is impossible by construction. A dev can still sell their own tokens on the curve, which the trust panel surfaces (Dev holds % / Dev exits).

What does it cost to launch?

Nothing but gas. There's no listing fee.

Why USDC for gas?

Arc is Circle's stablecoin L1 where the native gas token is USDC. One stable asset covers fees and trades — no second coin to acquire.

Do I keep control of my Google wallet?

Yes — it's a non-custodial embedded wallet; neither the platform nor the provider can move your funds. For a large or long-term stash, an injected wallet you fully control is still recommended.
